Australian Immigration

Australia is a majority migrant nation and one of the most successful and cohesive multicultural societies in the world. Migration is fundamental to the Australian story. Successive waves of migration have shaped and influenced the development of a rich multicultural society.

Past trend - The historic path to a multicultural Australia

According to the earliest population records that contain country of birth, in 1891, 32% of the population was born overseas. This reflects high levels of immigration in the late 19th century. The proportion fell to a low of 10% in 1947 due to decreased migration during World War I, the Great Depression, and World War II. The proportion then rose rapidly as a result of high levels of post-war migration. From 1971 to 2020, there was a more steady increase in the proportion of Australia’s population born overseas.

Generally, more people migrate to Australia than migrate away. This net flow of migrants contributes to the growth of Australia’s population. In 2021, the proportion of Australia's population born overseas declined. This is attributed to the COVID-19 pandemic travel restrictions and the resulting impact on overseas migration both to and from Australia. These travel restrictions were removed in 2022, and there was a subsequent increase in overseas migration into and out of Australia, most notably by people born overseas migrating to Australia.

Modernday statistics - Australian immigration seen from a global perspective

Australia is among the top 10 countries of raw number of immigration in the world, being comparable with other European countries such as France and Italy. After population normalisation, Australia with its small population moves up the ranks a bit, being comparable with Canada and Spain.

India, the UK and China accounted for more than one-third of the total immigrants in Australia, followed by Philippines and New Zealand.

The immigrants - The human aspects that form Australian cultures

Australia foreign population is dominated by young people from 18 to 30 years of age. Interestingly, that highest peak of 22-30 followed by 18-19 shows the two most common reasons for moving to Australia: after-graduate work and higher education.

Overall, it takes about 2-5 years for most to be granted permanent immigrant status. Interestingly, compared to other visas, more permanent Humanitarian are within the span of 3-5 years, suggesting that it is more common for Humantirian visa type to permanently settle ealier compared to other visa types.

Among permanent immigrants, people with Skilled visa have the highest proportion of having qualification, whereas Humanitarian ones the lowest. There is a strong balance between gender, and Bachelor Degree and Certificate III and IV (TAFE vocational training) are the most common qualifications.

State-level statistics - How different parts of Australia affect immigration

Most of foreign population decided the five major states to be their new place of settlement.

Immigrants from Asia, Middle East, Eastern Europe and Africa mostly prefer either New South Wales or Victoria, the two states that house two biggest cities of Australia. Immigrants from New Zealand, the UK and other Western European countries are more likely to immigrate to Queensland, Southern Australia and Western Australia.
